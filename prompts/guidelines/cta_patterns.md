# CTA Patterns

CTAs (calls to action) in Dave's content must feel earned, never forced. The primary function of content is to build trust and demonstrate expertise — not to sell. The CTA is the natural next step for a reader who already trusts what they just read.

---

## CTA Frequency Rules
- **Hard CTA** (direct ask for client work, DM, or service): Maximum 1 per 10 posts in the queue
- **Soft CTA** (implied value, no explicit ask): Maximum 1 per 5 posts
- **No CTA**: The majority of posts. No CTA needed. The value IS the CTA.

---

## Hard CTA Patterns (Agency — Use Sparingly)

Use only at the end of high-value thread breakdowns where the technical depth has clearly established authority. The CTA must connect directly to the content of the thread.

**Pattern A — DM trigger:**
"If you're building [specific type of thing] and running into [specific problem]: DM me 'crack' and let's talk."

**Pattern B — Direct offer:**
"I take on a few client builds a month. If this is the kind of problem you're sitting on, DM."

**Pattern C — Discovery:**
"Building something in this space? I'd be curious to hear what the actual blocker is. DM."

**What these have in common:** Specific, brief, no fake scarcity, no pressure, no emoji parade. The ask is one natural sentence.

---

## Soft CTA Patterns (Use at Moderate Frequency)

These don't ask for anything. They just leave a door open by demonstrating the value of working with someone who thinks this way.

**Pattern A — Implied capability:**
"This is the kind of thing that separates a working implementation from one that breaks under real load. Knowing which is which is a lot of what the work actually is."

**Pattern B — Social proof without bragging:**
"Took me two weeks to figure this out the first time. Now it's a one-day build."

**Pattern C — Expertise signal:**
"Most devs never get past the surface explanation. This is what's actually happening underneath."

---

## CTA Patterns for Replies

In replies, CTAs must be softer still. Replies are brand touchpoints, not sales calls.

**Allowed:**
- A demonstration of relevant capability, stated as information: "Built something similar for a client — the [specific decision] was the whole game."
- An implicit offer: "Happy to dig into this with you if you want to think it through."

**Not allowed:**
- Any direct sales ask in a reply
- Links to services or websites in replies (unless the conversation specifically asked)
- "Check out what I do" language

---

## CTA Patterns for Day Series Posts

The Day Series builds credibility over time. CTA in Day Series posts should be extremely rare — they undermine the authenticity of the build-in-public format. When a Day Series post does include a soft CTA, it should feel like a natural update: "This is the kind of project I'm now equipped to help others with" not "Hire me."

---

## CTA Kill List — Never Use These

- "Like and retweet if you found this helpful"
- "Follow for more content like this"
- "Save this for later"
- "Drop a comment below"
- "Check out my [link]" with no context
- "DM me your biggest challenge" (too broad)
- Any CTA with more than 2 emoji
- Any CTA that uses the word "exclusive", "limited", or "free" as a hook
