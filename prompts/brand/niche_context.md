# Niche Context — @daviderameh_

## Primary Niche
**Dev Cracking / Reverse Engineering / Indie Tools**

This is not a generic "learn to code" account. The niche is specifically: developers who want to understand how things actually work under the hood, build tools for themselves or small audiences, and do it without VC funding or a team of ten. The audience ranges from curious beginners who want to go deeper, to experienced devs who are tired of surface-level content.

---

## Niche Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: Reverse Engineering & Cracking
Content about deconstructing existing tools, APIs, products, and systems to understand their internals. This includes:
- How a popular app's feature actually works under the hood
- What an API is doing that its docs don't explain
- How to replicate a feature by studying network requests / source code
- "I broke X and here's what I learned"

**Relevance score target:** Extract content that contains non-obvious technical mechanisms, undocumented behavior, or counterintuitive architecture decisions.

### Pillar 2: Indie Dev / Build in Public
The journey of building tools, apps, and products as a solo developer. This includes:
- Milestone posts (first user, first dollar, first breakage in production)
- The reality of solo development vs. team development
- Specific decisions made and why
- What worked, what didn't, what's next

**Anchor format:** "Day X of Cracking Dev Challenges to [goal]" — this is the primary series format. Content here is always personal and progress-anchored.

### Pillar 3: Tools & Stack Intelligence
Critical evaluation of developer tools, frameworks, and workflows. Not reviews — analyses. What is this tool actually good for? Where does it fail? Who should and shouldn't use it?

**Extraction priority:** Content that challenges the mainstream narrative about a popular tool, or highlights a lesser-known tool that solves a real problem better.

### Pillar 4: Bold Dev Opinions
Contrarian positions on how developers should think, build, and grow. Anti-hype takes on industry trends. Challenges to widely-accepted "best practices" that don't hold up under scrutiny.

**Examples of high-relevance angles:**
- Why most "best practices" are team practices that don't apply to solos
- Why shipping ugly code that works beats architectural perfection
- Why the framework wars are irrelevant if you can't ship
- Why documentation is the actual moat

### Pillar 5: Agency & Freelance Reality
Content that bridges technical depth with the business of being a developer-for-hire. What clients actually want. What separates a developer who gets projects from one who doesn't. The funnel from technical credibility to paid work.

---

## Audience Profile

**Primary:** Indie developers, solo builders, junior-to-mid devs who want to go deeper technically. Age 18–35. Active on X, GitHub, Discord. Probably building something on the side.

**Secondary:** Tech founders and startup builders who are technical themselves or managing technical teams. They follow for the no-BS takes.

**What they're tired of:** Tutorial content that doesn't explain the why. Hype about AI tools that don't actually work. Advice written for FAANG engineers that doesn't apply to solo devs. Motivation content masquerading as technical content.

**What they want:** Someone who actually built something and is honest about how it went. Specific, actionable insights. The thing the tutorial skipped. The failure that led to the fix.

---

## Relevance Filter for Extraction

When scoring extracted points, prioritize content that:
- Challenges a widely-held assumption in the dev world (score +2)
- Contains a specific, non-obvious technical mechanism (score +2)
- Has a "here's what they don't tell you" angle (score +1)
- Is applicable to solo/indie devs specifically (score +1)
- Connects to the build-in-public journey (score +1)

Deprioritize content that:
- Is basic tutorial-level content (score -3)
- Is primarily about large-team engineering practices (score -2)
- Is generic productivity or motivation (score -2)
- Is purely marketing or PR content from a company (score -2)
