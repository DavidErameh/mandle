# Brand Rules — @daviderameh_

These are non-negotiable constraints. Any generated content that violates these rules must be revised or rejected. These rules exist to protect the brand's credibility, authenticity, and long-term trust with its audience.

---

## HARD RULES — NEVER DO THESE

### Rule 1: No Auto-Posting Voice
Never generate content that sounds like it was produced by a content machine. Specifically avoid:
- Starting posts with "Here are X ways to..."
- Using numbered tips as the primary content format for single posts (threads are okay)
- Generic productivity or motivation content that has no technical angle

### Rule 2: No Unearned Claims
Every specific claim must be grounded in something real. Do not generate posts that imply Dave built, tested, or experienced something he hasn't. Use hedged language when the source material is external: "From what I've read...", "The data suggests...", "Worth stress-testing this, but..."

### Rule 3: No Platform Shilling
Do not write content that reads as sponsored or promotional for any specific tool, platform, or service. The brand can reference tools — but always critically or in context of a real use case. Never use promotional language patterns ("Try X today", "Check out Y").

### Rule 4: No Character Count Padding
Do not inflate content to hit a length target. If a post is complete at 80 characters, it should be 80 characters. Padding destroys the voice. Quality over length, always.

### Rule 5: No Trend-Chasing Without Substance
Do not write about trending topics just because they're trending. The extraction pipeline should only surface content where Dave can add a genuine, specific angle. A post that exists only because something is viral is not a Dave post.

### Rule 6: No Attacks on Named Individuals
Do not generate content that calls out, criticizes, or names individual people negatively. Critiquing ideas, approaches, or industry patterns is fine and encouraged. Targeting people is not.

### Rule 7: No Filler Hashtags
Do not end posts with hashtag collections: #coding #developer #buildinpublic #indie. Maximum one hashtag per post, only when it meaningfully places the content in a specific community (e.g., posting in an X Community about reverse engineering).

### Rule 8: No Fabricated Metrics
Do not invent or estimate specific numbers unless they come from the source material. "I saved 3 hours" is only writable if the source establishes this. When no metric exists, use qualitative language.

---

## SOFT RULES — USE STRONG JUDGMENT

### Rule 9: Limit Emoji Use
Maximum 1–2 emoji per post. Emoji should emphasize, not decorate. A post with zero emoji is fine. A post with five emoji is not Dave.

### Rule 10: The Agency Funnel Is Subtle
CTA posts that direct to agency services should be rare (maximum 1 per 10 posts in the queue). The funnel works through authority and value, not through constant selling. When a CTA is used, it must feel earned by the content surrounding it.

### Rule 11: Tech References Must Be Accurate
When source material includes technical claims, do not simplify them into inaccuracies. It is better to be precise and slightly harder to read than to be breezy and wrong. The audience will notice.

### Rule 12: Sensitive Topics (Nigeria, Africa, Tech)
Content touching on regional tech ecosystems (Africa, Nigeria) is welcome and authentic to Dave's experience — but must be specific and grounded. Avoid generalizations. If the content is about building SkyNetGo or local infrastructure, it should reflect real-world specifics, not generic "Africa tech" narrative.
