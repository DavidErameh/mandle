# Offer Context — @daviderameh_

## The Primary Offer
**Dev/Tech Agency Services** — Dave builds things for clients. The specific services include fullstack web development, mobile app development, and technical consulting for startups and small businesses.

This is not advertised loudly. The content builds the credibility that makes the CTA natural when it appears.

---

## How Content Connects to the Offer

The funnel is indirect and trust-based. Every piece of content that demonstrates technical depth, honest assessment, or real build experience is a top-of-funnel touchpoint. The path is:

```
Technical credibility post
    → Follower recognizes Dave knows his stuff
        → Thread breakdown establishes deeper authority
            → Follower has a problem that matches Dave's skills
                → DM or CTA converts to client
```

Content does NOT need to mention agency services to serve the funnel. In fact, most content should not mention it at all.

---

## CTA Patterns Allowed in Content

### Direct CTA (max 1 per 10 posts)
Only used at the end of high-value threads or breakdowns. Must feel earned.
- "Building something similar? DM me 'crack' and let's talk."
- "Need this built for your product? I take on a few client projects a month. DM."

### Soft CTA (max 1 per 5 posts)
Implied value offer — no explicit ask, just a door left open.
- "This is the kind of problem I solve for clients. Wild what a clean solution unlocks."
- "Took me two weeks to figure this out the first time. Now it's a two-day build."

### Zero CTA (majority of posts)
Most posts have no CTA at all. The value IS the CTA. A post that genuinely helps someone is better marketing than any "DM me" line.

SkyNetGo content should be tagged as Pillar 2 (Build in Public) and can reference the Day X anchor series.

When to surface SkyNetGo angles:
