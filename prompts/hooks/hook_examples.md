# Hook Examples — In Dave's Voice

These are example hooks written in Dave's actual voice. Use these to calibrate tone, not to copy. They are illustrations of what the voice sounds like when the hook templates are applied correctly.

---

## Blunt Fact — Examples
- "Most API rate limits aren't about protecting the server. They're about protecting the business model."
- "The bug that takes 4 hours to find is almost never where you first looked."
- "Documentation lies. Not maliciously. It just doesn't age well and nobody updates it."

---

## Specific Failure — Examples
- "Spent two days optimizing a query that wasn't the bottleneck. The actual bottleneck was a missing index that took 4 minutes to add."
- "Rebuilt the auth flow from scratch. The first version worked. The rebuilt version was worse. Classic."
- "Thought the API was broken. It was my assumption about the API that was broken."

---

## Contrarian Opening — Examples
- "Clean code is a team convention, not a universal law. When you're shipping solo, readable > clean."
- "The advice to 'talk to your users' is correct. The timeline people give it — before you've built anything — is wrong."
- "Most 'productivity' tools for developers are complexity generators dressed as simplifiers."

---

## Day Series — Examples
- "Day 23 of cracking the first $1k MRR. The pricing page is live. Feels too cheap and too expensive simultaneously."
- "Day 7. The Starlink setup is working. The MikroTik config is not. One step forward."
- "Day 31 of cracking dev challenges. Shipped the media picker. It's not beautiful but it works and that's the whole game right now."

---

## Curiosity Gap — Examples
- "Thought the bottleneck was the database. It wasn't. It was the thing right before the database."
- "The framework I avoided for two years because of the ecosystem complaints actually solved the specific problem I had."
- "Asked a senior dev why they still use the 'old' approach for this. Their answer made everything click."

---

## Direct Opinion — Examples
- "Tailwind is overrated for solo devs. You spend more time remembering class names than you do solving actual problems."
- "Build in public posts that only show wins are just marketing. The builds people actually learn from show the inventory of what broke."
- "The gap between devs who ship and devs who don't has almost nothing to do with technical skill."

---

## Specific Observation — Examples
- "Every project I've seen die in the side-project graveyard died at the same point: after the first version was working but before the second version was useful."
- "The pattern across every dev who's successfully freelanced: they stopped selling their skills and started selling specific outcomes."
- "There's a thing that happens when you switch from 'what should I build' to 'what specific problem am I solving' — the scope collapses in the best way."

---

## Before/After — Examples
- "Before I understood how the API actually authenticated: 3 hours of debugging. After: 20-minute integrations."
- "Old approach: build the full feature, then test it. Current approach: test the ugly version first, then finish it."
- "Six months ago I was copy-pasting Stack Overflow. Now I'm reading the source. The difference in what I can build is significant."
